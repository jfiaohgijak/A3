Skills and Jobs

Project Manager

A project manager is one of the most important roles when starting any project, a project manager plays the lead role in planning, executing, monitoring, controlling and closing out projects. Project Managers are accountable for the entire project scope, project team and resources., project budget and the success or failure of the project in general. By having a project manager apart of this project, this will ensure the smooth development of the application and that all bases are covered with other team members such as customer support staff, UI designers and developers. This project manager must be able to be strategic is the operations of this project, be able to encourage and recognise the value that teams members bring to the table, fully vested in the success of the project and most of all take accountability. 

Lead Developer

The primary goal of a lead developer is to direct and guide the application team in design, coding, testing and researching within the project. A lead developer needs to be a problem solver and be open to other’s interpretations and solutions to problem. By having a lead developer within this project, this will give the ability for other roles such as UI designer, to be supported and guided during the project timeline and lend out a helping hand if needed. The lead developer can also help the Project Manager in overcoming any problems such as budget costs or development issues that tend to be discovered during the project. 

UI/UX Developer

The role of a UI/UX designer is to create a user-interface for an application, website, or other interactive media for users. Ther work includes collaborating with product managers to understand the needs of the users to therefore gather requirements and designing an interface that would best suit the client. UI/UX designer usually have a high level of creative ability and think outside of the box when coming up with a solution to their problem. Ultimately, it is their job to create functional and appealing features for users to interact with. In this project, a UI/UX designer will be utilised in designing the main interfaces of the application and work closely with the lead developer in designing an interface that not only looks good but is able to incorporate all the functional requirements presented by the project shareholders.  

Customer Service Officer

A customer service officer act as the face of company and directly work with customers/clients in resolving issues or queries and providing additional support when required.  Those that work well with people and are able to communicate and act accordingly with customers have the greatest chance at obtaining these positions. A customer service officer would fit well within the staff for this project as they might be the head of user-testing, with their ability to communicate well and understand the frustrations and difficulties that users’/clients face, this information can then be used to further develop and improve the developing application. Not only with the service officer be useful during the project, but will also be help once the project has been completed and application released to the wider public, with a customer service officer that has been involved with the project from the beginning, they will have a great idea of the various issues that user may face and then can understand and report back on any findings to people such as the lead developer and/or project manager.
