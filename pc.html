Group Processes and Communication

How will your group communicate? 

Our group has decided to hold meeting on Microsoft teams, some sessions will be ones where we spend a few hours working through the project, others will be check-ins to see how far we are to completing. Another form of communication is over Facebook Messenger where we can quickly and easily post queries and check-ins for parts of the assignment and other things such as collaboration. 

Will meetings be face-to face or using technologies?  

A few of our earlier meetings were face-to-face when we had a practical class, but then as the weeks went on, we moved to using Microsoft Teams as our main port of call and in these meetings, we would work together and check-in on how the project was progressing. 

What will you do if a group member doesn’t respond? 

If a group member doesn’t respond we will have to move on and continue without their input. Considering we have all the work on a shared platform, not work will be lost, the only thing that would be lost is the help from the additional team member, however, in our group all group members have been very active and dedicated to their tasks despite distractions and other Uni assessments.  

Overall 

Overall, our group was always communicating with each other and that meant that we were able to start off with a good foundation from the beginning of this project. Although sometimes we fell behind because of the lack of communication, we all encouraged each other to push on and finish any tasks that needed to be completed.
